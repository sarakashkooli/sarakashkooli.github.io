About & Research Statement

Research Pathway

My research journey began with co-authoring systematic reviews and meta-analyses of clinical trials for metabolic diseases, resulting in 14 publications (including 5 in Q1 journals)[cite: 6]. Over the past five years, my continuous role as a clinical diet therapy consultant in hospital settings has provided me with deep practical insights into patient trajectories and chronic conditions[cite: 7].

Future Vision & Core Ideas

Moving forward, my research goal is to transition from clinical nutrition to large-scale nutritional epidemiology[cite: 8]. I aim to leverage whole-of-population linked data and prospective cohorts to investigate the longitudinal trajectories of chronic diseases, diet-gene interactions, and their impacts on healthy ageing[cite: 9]. I am particularly eager to apply advanced statistical modeling and programming (Python/R) to generate data-driven public health evidence[cite: 10, 136].

Programming in Science

Automating Unit Standardization for Meta-Analyses Using Python

Extracting data from clinical trials often presents researchers with diverse reporting units (e.g., mg/dL vs. mmol/L). In this article, I discuss how to build custom Python pipelines with Pandas and NumPy to automate unit standardization...

When executing systematic reviews, one of the most tedious steps is manual unit conversion. Leveraging my programming background, I developed a lightweight script that standardizes biochemical marker data extracted from diverse studies automatically.

def standardize_lipid_units(value, unit, analyte):
    if analyte == "cholesterol" and unit == "mg/dL":
        return value * 0.02586  # convert to mmol/L
    return value

Implementing this workflow reduces data-entry errors and accelerates high-impact research, a practice I applied across my 14 publications.

Nutritional Epidemiology

The Imperative of Long-term Cohorts in Healthy Ageing

Clinical trials provide isolated insights, but to understand the trajectory of healthy aging, nutritional epidemiology must rely on prospective, large-scale cohorts. How do cumulative diet patterns impact lifespan?...

To tackle chronic metabolic conditions, we must move beyond short-term interventions. Cohorts allow us to study the interactions of lifestyle and biology across decades. Understanding diet as a longitudinal exposure is key to shifting public policy from reactive disease management to proactive prevention.

Nutrigenetics

Bridging the Gap Between Diet-Gene Interactions and Public Health

Are general dietary guidelines truly effective? This post reviews how polymorphisms (such as FTO) influence individuals' metabolic responses and why personalized clinical trials are the future of epidemiology...

Nutrigenetics represents the frontier of modern preventive medicine. My early literature work on FTO polymorphism showed clear modulations in Leptin levels. Integrating genomics into large epidemiology frameworks will define the next generation of precise clinical diets.
